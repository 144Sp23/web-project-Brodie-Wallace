President | Brenden Engeman

Serves as the chief executive officer of IFC. He presides over all meetings of the Board and the Delegation. He act as the official delegate of IFC at all events and/or meetings both within and outside the University.

Vice President of Membership Development | Jarrot Simon

Shall assume all Presidential powers in the absence or disability of thePresident. He shall oversee the Membership and Development committee and meet with members at least twice a trimester. Shall be responsible for supervising and promoting IFCrecruitment.

Vice President of Judicial Affairs | Carson Seibert

Shall recommend the form of sanctions to the Board for those IFC fraternities failing to comply with Article IV of theConstitution. Shall approve and oversee the judicial delegates appointed by each IFC fraternity.

Vice President of Communications and Programming | Brodie Wallace

Shall oversee the Communications and Programming committee and meet withmembers at least twice a trimester. Shall coordinate with Panhellenic executive board member in charge of public relations and programming, to plan one Greek speakers once per trimester. Shall work with IFC advisor on publications.

Vice President of Philantropy and Service | Salvatore Bonadonna

Shall be responsible for coordinating a philanthropic event in the spring trimester that is open to Greek Life. Shall assist in coordinating a philanthropic event in the fall trimester with the Panhellenic Council VP of Philanthropy and Service. Shall work with the Maryville community to provide service opportunities for each chapter and members.

Vice President of Scholarchip and Academic Development | Jackson Clouse

Shall oversee the Scholarship Committee and meet with members at least twice a trimester. Shall be responsible for the process of annually awarding the Outstanding New Member Scholarship and the Outstanding Active Member Scholarship.Shall be responsible for distribution of all IFC awards as outlined by Article VIof these IFC Bylaws.

Vice President of Risk Management | Robbie Keyes

Shall educate each chapter on IFC, and Northwest Missouri State University policies once a trimester. Host at least one Risk Management Educational Session per trimester. Shall be a resource to fraternities in ways to reduce their overall liability.
